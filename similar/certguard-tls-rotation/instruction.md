The TLS estate on one of our edge hosts is in a bad way after a migration, and I need it reconciled before the next audit. Every internal service on that box terminates TLS with a certificate bound to it in the host's inventory, and those bindings have rotted: some leaves are past their renewal window, one was pulled after a key-compromise report, and several now chain up through intermediates or roots we no longer trust. certguard, under /app, is what should walk that inventory and settle each service — rotate it onto a still-valid replacement already staged on the host, leave a healthy one alone, or take it offline when nothing trustworthy covers it — except the copy on the box is only half-written and its verdicts can't be relied on.

Everything certguard decides from lives under /app/data/inventory: the certificates staged on the host, the trust anchors we accept, the certificate each service is currently bound to, and a policy that fixes the instant every decision is evaluated against. Revocation is the one thing it can't read locally — it has to ask the service at REVOCATION_API_BASE, once per issuer it needs to check. Out of that it has to reach the right verdict for each service and write the configuration that enforces it: an nginx snippet for each rotation, a systemd drop-in for each service it disables, a canonical JSON trust report, and a Markdown audit.

What makes a verdict correct — how a chain is built and validated up to a trusted anchor, how wildcard and name-constrained identities are matched, when a CRL counts as fresh and what within it counts as revoked, how the replacement is chosen, and how a certificate's fingerprint is computed — is written out in /app/docs/cert-spec.md, together with the exact byte layout of every artifact; treat that document as the contract. certguard is built from its sources under /app and recompiled before it runs, so the repair has to live in the code rather than the files it emits, and /app/bin/certguard has to produce contract-correct artifacts for whatever inventory and revocation data it is aimed at.
