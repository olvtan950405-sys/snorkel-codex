# Repair the ForgeGate OCI provenance admission gate

The disconnected deployment mirror at `/app` relies on `forgegate` to decide which locally staged OCI images may cross into production. The replacement copy completes every run and writes convincing admission records, but it trusts index annotations, accepts attestations for the wrong manifest, and applies vulnerability waivers far beyond their intended scope.

Repair the Ruby implementation so `/app/bin/forgegate evaluate --layout <directory> --policy <json-file> --keyring <json-file> --waivers <json-file> --out <directory>` authenticates the selected platform manifests and their provenance before producing deployment or quarantine records. It must resolve OCI descriptors from their content-addressed bytes, validate the complete index-to-manifest-to-config/layer graph, verify distinct authorized Ed25519 attestations over their exact canonical payloads, bind each provenance subject to the selected manifest digest, enforce builder/source/ref/commit and build-time policy, and apply only vulnerability waivers whose identity, package, advisory, platform and time scope all match.

`/app/docs/admission-contract.md` defines the supported OCI profile, evidence format, validation and rejection precedence, policy boundaries, and exact output artifacts. Treat it as authoritative, including its distinction between malformed provenance and structurally valid evidence that violates semantic policy. Keep the repair in `/app/lib`; the verifier invokes `/app/bin/forgegate` directly as an executable against newly generated layouts and policies, so preserve that executable path and permission rather than relying on `ruby /app/bin/forgegate`. Editing `/app/data` or generated output cannot solve the task. The result must remain offline and deterministic.
